Several of these shells display a second pattern at lower pigmentation densities. In the  shell below this faint pattern maintains the character of the main pattern



In contrast, in the two below this has more of an area-filling character. While the onset of pigmentation is sharp, the switching of is obviously irregular, leading to the somewhat fuzzy appearance. It is not clear whether this background pattern results from a partially independent system that forms in a deeper layer of the mantle gland, or whether pattern formation can proceed at such a reduced level. The systematic decrease of pigmentation levels in the figure below suggests the second possibility. In many cases the reduced pigmentation spreads with a reduced speed
 


The formation of a background pattern occurs also in other shells.  Oliva porphyria is an example.

Two organizing regions at opposite sides of a field:
hydra head and foot formation as example

In many embryonic fields each terminal position carries a specific organizing region. Hydra is no exception. In addition to the head on one side, the foot is formed at the other. Which mechanism causes the two organizing regions to appear at opposite positions? Simple cross-inhibition of a head system and a foot system is not appropriate since in small (young) animals head and foot must appear very close together. At such a short distance a direct mutual inhibition between both systems would lead to the suppression of foot regeneration by the nearby head.

It rather appears that it is the competence introduced above that plays the crucial role in the positioning of the two organizers. As mentioned, the competence for head formation is a graded tissue property and the head signal stabilizes this competence. We assume, therefore, that the foot system has the opposite behavior: it appears in the region of lowest head-forming competence (lowest source density; blue distribution in the simulations). Thus, the foot signal (black in the simulation) forms at the maximum distance from the head signal (green). In small animals the head and the foot system can coexist at a close neighborhood since there is no direct inhibition between the two centers. The graded source density only generates a preference for the terminal positions. In the simulation below, after removal of the foot, a new foot signal (black) regenerates close to the head signal. The trigger of the new foot signal is without any influence on the head signal (during the regeneration, the foot inhibitor is shown as a brown line):

The second organizer is assumed to contribute to the maintenance of tissue polarity. While the head signal appears, preferentially, at the region of highest competence and increases the competence, the foot signal has the opposite properties: it appears at the lowest head competence and lowers this competence even further. Low competence for head formation is equivalent to a high competence for foot formation. The foot enhances this foot-competence by lowering the competence for head formation.

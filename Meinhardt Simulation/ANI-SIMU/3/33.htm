Oscillations in an activator-substrate interaction

Likewise, oscillations are also possible in an activator-substrate system. They occur if the production rate of the substrate (bb in equation 2.4) is too low to maintain the production of the activator at a steady state. This is the case if the production rate of the substrate is lower than the decay rate of the activator. In terms of equation 2.4, the condition is bb < ra (see equation below).
 

In contrast,  with bb = 0.1 and keeping ra = 0.04, after some oscillations the system enters into a stable state:
 
 


In this simulation, the removal of the substrate occurs exclusively by the consumption due to the self-activation. If there is an additional decay (rb>0), the substrate concentration may remain too low for a spontaneous trigger and the system may become excitable. It the activator concentration is low, it would remain low until  a trigger occurred. This can lead to traveling waves, as shown on the next page.

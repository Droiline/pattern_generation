Pattern elements that are correctly described details



The simulations reproduce many details of the natural pattern. The mechanisms were designed to satisfy the simultaneous branch initiation. The high probability of simultaneous initiation leads to many V-like elements with the same distance between the tip of the V and the points of branch initiation:


Many branches appear only loosely connected with the oblique line. Sometimes a small hook appears close to the point of branch initiation:
 



This indicates that the original wave proceeds undisturbed. In contrast, between the primary activation and the activation of the backwards- running wave a temporary depression can exist. In the model, this effect results from an overshoot during the normal chain of triggering events, which leads to concentrations much higher than the temporary steady state. Moreover, most of the pulse-like activation may already be over when the transition towards the temporary steady state occurs. Thus, the local re-activation can occur after some delay, leading to a small gap between the primary oblique line and the branch. The small hooks result from incipient waves initiated parallel to the primary wave. Frequently these waves don't survive since the cells are not yet sufficiently excitable. However, if this wave does survive a parallel line close to the original line is formed:
 



Very dense local branching can occur. In the model this occurs if by chance many waves become extinct almost simultaneously. A low density of waves in some parts along the growing edge causes an increase in branching in the remaining parts. Branching can occur in rapid succession.
 


    


The model outlined so far does not allow the spontaneous trigger of new waves. However, the spontaneous initiation of pairs of pigment lines after long non-activated periods indicate that this occurs in reality. This requires that an inhibitor drop to very low levels such that a new activation can be triggered from a basic activator level. However, the fact that the waves don't change the speed or the level of activation even after a long non-activated period clearly indicates that this cannot result from the same inhibitor that is employed so far for switching off the activation. Thus, a second inhibitor must exist that has a long time constant. In the simulation shown below, this inhibitor has also a longer range. With this addition, the pattern can be generated from an initially uniform situation. The existence of two antagonistic reactions has been also deduced from completely different shell patterns (chapter 5)

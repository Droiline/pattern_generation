Shift and split of maxima and lines - the role of saturation

If the autocatalysis saturates at high levels of the self-enhancing loop, the maximum broadens: if the peak height is restricted, the spatial extension of the maximum increases until the corresponding amount of inhibitor is produced. In the two simulations below the saturation is introduced during the simulation. The broadening of peaks is clearly visible in both the movie and in the space-time plot.  The extension of the maxima depends on the extension of the regions into which the inhibitor can diffuse.
 
Such saturation strongly influences the behavior of the system. The maxima become more regular since they shift more easily. If two peaks are too close together, the side close to another peak will become deactivated while those on the other side cells become newly activated while the size of the total activated region is maintained. This is clearly visible in the simulations above. After the onset of the saturation the activation spreads more extensively into the larger gap at the center.

The simulations shown below show the effect of saturation. Except the saturation (sa in the equation below), they are made with the same parameters. Even the same random fluctuations have been used. With saturation (right) peaks are broader and appear much closer together. Note that due to the limitation in the autocatalysis it takes longer for the distributions to resolve into separated maxima.

With saturation, the absolute peak height is much reduced. Below the final patterns of the simulations shown above are plotted at the same scale:



Saturation can lead to a split of a maximum into two. In the following simulation, the saturation is increased after a certain time interval. The center of the widening maximum has more problems to get rid of the inhibitor. The flanks are in a much better situation. The autocatalysis collapses in the center but survives at the two flanks. This leads to a split of a maximum. Their movement leads to a roughly equal spacing from each other and from the border. Since the peaks move more easily, systems with saturating autocatalysis show usually a more regular spacing.

Lines in pairs: Survival at the flanks

On some shells stripes are formed in pairs:
 
  


This pattern can be explained by an early switching off of the communication between the cells. Cells in the center of the maximum are exposed to the highest inhibition that can no longer escape. They become de-activated. A basal inhibitor production (bb>0) keeps the activation low in the non-activated regions.


This type of double stripe formation is restricted to the outside pattern of the shell. Most unusual, this mollusk also forms a pattern on the inner side of the shell. There, however, the spacing remains normal:

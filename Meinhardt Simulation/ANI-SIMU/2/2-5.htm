The activator-depleted substrate model

The antagonistic reaction can proceed at the expense of a substrate or co-factor that is derived from a larger surrounding region. The substrate concentration becomes smallest at the position of an activator maximum:


The low substrate concentration at the position of a maximum restricts the maximum activator production. If locally substrate is no longer available, no further increase of the activator production is possible. Thus, an upper bound of the activator concentation and a saturation of the autocatalys is an inherent feature of the activator-depletion mechanism. Therefore, as in the activator-inhibitor mechanism with saturation, a tendency for shifting and splitting of maxima exists (compare with the activator-inhibitor mechanism with saturation). The width of the stripes increases and becomes comparable to the distances between the stripes.




The activator-depleted substrate model is, therefore, inconvenient to model situations where sharp peaks have to dominate over large non-activated regions as it is required, for example,  for the formation of organizing regions.

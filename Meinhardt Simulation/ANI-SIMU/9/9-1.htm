Parallel lines with „tongues“:
an oscillating and a stable system cooperate
 

The shells shown above are decorated with many fine parallel lines. This suggests that synchronous oscillations took place. At particular positions, however, these parallel lines are interrupted and form U- or V- shaped gaps.  Such 'tongues' result from the temporary suspension of otherwise almost synchronous oscillations. A non-pigmented area results that is re-pigmented from neighboring regions in which oscillations survived. The V- or U-shaped oblique lines that border the tongues indicate that traveling waves move into the regions of interrupted oscillations, filling the gaps by revitalizing the oscillations. After two such waves meet, normal oscillation usually continues, at least for a certain interval.

An important hint in determining the underlying mechanism comes from a background pigmentation that is visible on some shells. As can be seen on the shells shown below, the background pigmentation exists between the parallel lines but is absent in the tongues. After the last pulse of the pigmentation system before the onset of tongue formation, the gray background pigmentation is still present for a time that is comparable with the cycle time of the oscillating system:
 
 

Based on this background pattern, the following mechanism was proposed: the background system is a prerequisite for the oscillating system. The oscillating system, in turn, acts to refresh the background system. When a pulse of the oscillating system appears too late, the background system and thus the prerequisite for the sustained oscillations switches off. After each pulse, some sort of race takes place. Only if the next pulse is not too late, it can accomplish the refresh. The mutual dependence of a steady state system and an oscillating system may be illustrated with an analogy. The living state of a higher organism depends on a beating heart. But the heart can only beat if the organism is alive. If a single heartbeat comes too late, the organism may die. Of course, if the organism dies for other reasons, the heart will also stop beating.

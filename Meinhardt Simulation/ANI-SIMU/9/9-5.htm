The variability in the Clithon-type patterning

The Clithon shells show huge pattern diversity. Many of these patters still wait for an appropriate simulation. Some of the shells below have shown earlier to justify the model approach.

The first three shells below illustrate that the oscillation frequency can be very different, ranging from a steady state and thus of a homogeneous covering with pigment to a wider spacing. On the right shell, the horizontal lines almost merge to a homogeneous light-colored background and only the borders of the tongues are stronger pigmented.

..



The pattern forming system can have a different susceptibility for the interruption of the synchronous oscillations and gap formation. On the shell at left, only rarely gaps are formed and this restricted to particular zones. In contrast, at the shell at right, tongue formation occurs in rapid succession.

.



The restriction of the synchronous oscillations to particular regions can be profound. On the left shell, these zones are connected with oblique lines. On the central cells, were this oblique lines meet, the pigmentation becomes reduced and of different color. At the right shell, these connections no longer exist.

  



After tongue formation, the regions in which the oscillation takes place, enlarges. A traveling wave (oblique line) paves the area in which oscillations become possible again. The triangular shape of the pigmented regions on the left shell has also this origin. This alternation between spread and retraction is the same as discussed above for moving gaps, where the spread can take place on one side of the tongue while on the other retraction occurs (shell in the center). The alternation between spread and retraction is not obligatory. It occurs in a marginal band on the shell at right but not in the central band. The shell at left shows also that formation of oblique lines (traveling waves) can still proceed when parallel lines (synchronous oscillations) are no longer possible.
 
 

  

Within different bands, the pigmentation may differ dramatically. On both shells below, the marginal bands are completely filled while the central regions show the usual patterns of parallel lines and gaps:

 




After a larger gap has been formed, the pigment system may be able to proceed only through a few oscillations. This is the case in a particular region on the shell at left, leading to the repetition of one or a few fine lines after a gap. In terms of the model, the precondition for the oscillation is restored only for a short period. On the shell in the center, a larger gap is followed a very thick black region. From the model it is expected that during the arrest of the oscillation in the gap-phase, the cell becomes more excitable. Either an inhibitor decreases to very low level or the substantial accumulation of a substrate could be responsible for the increased excitability. That the activation within the traveling wave during gap closure is stronger than those in the synchronous oscillations is also visible on the shell at right. While the oblique lines are black, the weaker pigmentation of the parallel lines leads to a change in color, from black to red or pink.

The modifying pattern is stable: fishbone-like pattern

The shell of Volutoconus bednalli shows isolated perpendicular pigmentation lines that are connected with oblique lines.

This suggests that the enhancing reaction is stable in time. Lines perpendicular to the growing edge are formed from which traveling waves spread in both directions. Their V-shape results from normal annihilation of colliding waves.

 
a,b: Activator-substrate system producing the pigment, 
c, d: is an activator-inhibitor system that stabilizes the pigmentation system by increasing the substrate production (via sb). Since the c-autocatalysis depends on a, high c levels can emerge only at high a levels. In contrast to the simulation of the previous page, c is generating a stable pattern (since rd>rc). Due to the elevated substrate production, the pigmentation also remains stable in regions of high c. The local stability depends on the mutual stabilization of both systems.

The modifying pattern keeps the pigmentation reaction close to the steady state

A further mode of the enhancing reaction is suggested by the pattern on Cymbiola verpertilio and Cymbiola nobilis. The darker pigmented regions show a slight modulation by horizontal lines:

This suggests that a modulating pattern exists that oscillates. Each pulse contributes to maintaining the steady state of the pigmentation system. In the lower part part of the simulation, shown below, only the resulting pigmentation pattern is plotted. In the upper part the modulating pattern is shown in addition.

The darker pigmentation occurs preferentially only in two bands. This suggests that the substrate production of the pigment reaction is spatially modulated. The two figures below show the same simulation, one as space-time plot and one as an animated sequence of concentrations at the growing edge.

 
a , b: activator (brown) -substrate system producing the pigment, 
c, d: activator (green)-inhibitor system that stabilizes the pigmentation system by producing additional substrate (via sb). On their own, both systems would oscillate since bb<ra and rd < rc. The additional substrate, produced under influence of the modifying pattern c keeps the pigmentation system close to the steady state but larger breakdowns do occur. The preferential formation of dark regions in two bands is achieved by a corresponding  modulation of substrate production rate bb(x). Since c diffuses rapidly, the oscillations of the modifying reaction synchronize

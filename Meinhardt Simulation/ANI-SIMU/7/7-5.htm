The modifying pattern oscillates: Conus textile





Conus textile shows parallel lines on a dark background and finer branched lines on a white background. The dark regions are essentially confined to two regions on the shell.




In the model, whether the enhancing reaction leads to dark lines on a pigmented background or to the mesh-like pattern on a white background depends on whether the pigmentation reaction is in a steady state (below left) or not (center). In the fist case, the enhancing reaction leads to the dark stripes, in the second to branch formation.  Therefore, assumed was a position-dependent substrate production (right). This leads to regions in which either the one or the other pattern elements dominate.
 
 

 
 
a,b: Activator-substrate system producing the pigment, 
c, d: is an activator-inhibitor system that stabilizes the pigmentation system. At the simulation top left, the pigmentation reaction is in a steady state (bb > ra), in the center it is oscillating (bb < ra). The figure on the right results from a corresponding  modulation of bb (as indicated at the top of the figure)
Da = 0.01; ra = 0.05; ba = 0; sa =0.01; s = ra + 5% random fluctuation; Db = 0; rb = 0.0045; bb = 0.06 (left) or 0.03 (center); sb = 0.5
Dc=0.005; rc=0.01; bc = 0.005; Dd = 0.4; rd=0.014; sd = 0.01


In the following animated sequence, the maintenance of the pigment reaction (brown) on the left side and the traveling waves on the right are clearly visible. The enhancing reaction (green) leads either to an enhancement of the steady state activation (left) or to the temporary transition into a steady state and thus to the initiation of branches (right).

Essential elements are reproduced. For instance, the dark horizontal lines are frequently the starting points of  the oblique lines on the white background. This is because the temporary high level of the pigment activator triggers a traveling wave with high probability.

.




However, a comparison of the simulated with the real pattern also reveals differences. Even in zones that show high background pigmentation over extended periods, this background pattern suddenly disappears, leading to the white triangles in the dark regions. This shows once again the close connection between enhancing and extinguishing components of the modifying reaction.
